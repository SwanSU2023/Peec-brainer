# Peec Brain — From LLM signal to executable actions

**Peec tells you what's happening in LLMs. Peec Brain tells you why, and what to do.**

Built in 48h for the [Peec MCP Challenge](https://peec.ai) · `#BuiltWithPeec` · submitted in **Content Optimization** + **Competitive Analysis**.

Stride Up pilots 40+ Peec AI accounts (Lancôme, Kérastase, FnacDarty, Audi, L'Oréal Professionnel, Nacon, Cabaia, Nat & Nin, and others). Every Monday, our consultants face the same four questions per account. Nobody answers them in one pipeline. We built one.

## Module 3 — Structural Audit (the differentiator)

**Why the brand page isn't cited, at two structural layers.**

Most competitors in this challenge stop at "here are the pages with low visibility." Peec Brain goes one step deeper and diagnoses the *structural* reason the brand page is ignored, along two axes:

**Layer A — Editorial structure.** Parses Peec `get_url_content` markdown for: H2/H3 density, named active ingredients, expert quote presence (MD, FAAD, DO), comparison tables, per-product standardised sections, TOC/anchors, `How we tested` and `Meet the experts` signals.

**Layer B — Technical structure (schema.org).** Parses raw HTML with `extruct` for JSON-LD, microdata, RDFa, Open Graph. Scores presence of `Product`, `AggregateRating`, `Review`, `FAQPage`, `HowTo`, `BreadcrumbList`, `Article`, `Organization`.

Diff = signals present on a majority of LLM-cited pages but absent on the brand page → prescriptive actions.

### Real run — Lancôme on *"Quel est le meilleur sérum anti-âge en 2026 selon les dermatologues ?"*

Lancôme's AI visibility on this prompt: **0 %**. ChatGPT cites 8 competitors and 15 source domains in its place.

| URL | citation_rate | H2 | ingredients | experts | table | JSON-LD types |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `lancome.fr/soin/par-categorie/serum-visage` | **0.00** | **0** | **0** | **0** | **—** | `BreadcrumbList, ListItem, Organization, WebPage` |
| `vogue.fr/beaute/article/meilleurs-serums-anti-age` | 0.36 | 11 | 12 | 1 | — | `AggregateRating, Article, Product, Review, Rating, Person` + 3 more |
| `healthline.com/.../best-anti-aging-serum` | **1.00** | 8 | 17 | 2 | ✓ | `FAQPage, ItemList, Product, Question, Answer` + 7 more |
| `vogue.co.uk/article/best-anti-ageing-serums` | 0.88 | 11 | 12 | 2 | — | `AggregateRating, Article, Product, Review, Rating, Person` + 3 more |

**Verdict generated by Peec Brain**: *"Major structural gaps detected: 3 editorial, 4 schema. LLMs do not find the expected signal on the brand page — they fall back on the 3 editorial pages cited instead."*

**Prescriptive actions** (7 emitted, excerpt):
1. Add at least 4 substantive H2 sections on the target page.
2. Explicitly name active ingredients: hyaluronic acid, ascorbate, ceramide, collagen, retinol, peptides…
3. Include 1-2 dermatologist expert quotes (with MD/FAAD credibility explicitly visible).
4. Implement JSON-LD `Product` on product and category pages.
5. Expose `AggregateRating` (rating value + review count) in JSON-LD.
6. Add `Review` JSON-LD for product reviews.
7. Restructure the page as `Article` with author, dateModified, and reviewedBy.

Full output: [`outputs/lancome_m3_structural_audit_serum.md`](outputs/lancome_m3_structural_audit_serum.md)

### Running Layer B live (outside sandbox)

Peec's `get_url_content` strips JSON-LD markup. To run Layer B on live URLs:

```bash
python3 scripts/fetch_and_audit.py \
  --urls https://www.healthline.com/health/beauty-skin-care/best-anti-aging-serum \
         https://www.vogue.co.uk/article/best-anti-ageing-serums \
  --out fixtures/live_schema.json
```

## Module 4 — Citation Authority Audit

**Who decided not to talk about you, and who they cite in your place.**

The content-gap stack of the challenge is crowded (Ritwika, Lukas, Drift Radar all ship variants of "pages with low visibility"). Nobody ships this: the domain-level mirror of the same gap — the editorial sources whose writers trust your competitors but have never sent a journalist to your brand.

### Pipeline

1. Peec `get_domain_report` filtered by prompt_id → cited domains + `mentioned_brand_ids[]` + Peec classification (EDITORIAL / UGC / CORPORATE / …).
2. Build **gap list** = domains citing at least one tracked competitor but never the own brand.
3. Enrich with Ahrefs `site-explorer-domain-rating` → DR + Ahrefs rank per domain.
4. Classify domain typology via curated whitelist + Peec classification fallback: `editorial_premium` / `editorial_health` / `specialized` / `retailer` / `ugc` / `reference` / `corporate`.
5. Priority score = `(citation_count + retrieval_count × 0.3) × (DR/100) × typology_weight`.
6. Emit per-typology outreach action. Export CSV sorted by priority.

**15 cited domains · 11 GAP domains · 5 HIGH priority targets:**

## Module 2 — Content Gap (traffic-first prioritization)

**Your page at 1 169 visites/mois that ChatGPT never cites. That one first.**

Several other submissions already ship content-gap analyzers (Ritwika's `workflow-1`, Lukas's `content-gap-hunt`, the Drift Radar "silence split"). Our angle: we don't just return the pages with 0% visibility, we **prioritize by existing business value** — `priority_score = Ahrefs top-page traffic × (1 − AI visibility)`.

The signal: a page already earning 1 000+ monthly visits on Google, but invisible in LLMs, is where every euro of optimization work should go first.

### Real run — Lancôme

**20 pages prioritized. Top gap:** `lancome.fr/beauty-magazine/la-routine-skincare-parfaite-pour-ma-peau.html` — 1 169 monthly organic visits on Google, **0 % AI visibility on 5 distinct Peec prompts**. Peec Brain ranks it before any low-traffic page and emits the exact optimization action.

Full output: [`outputs/lancome_content_gaps.csv`](outputs/lancome_content_gaps.csv)

Integrated Ahrefs enrichment: per-URL `sum_traffic`, `top_keyword`, `top_keyword_best_position`.

## Module 1 — Prompt Discovery (fusion GSC + Ahrefs + product catalog)

**The prompts you should be tracking — that you're not.**

Alexander Gumtz's `gsc2peecai` already occupies the pure GSC → Peec axis with a dual-threshold archiving pattern and SISTRIX volumes. Our angle is a different one: we **fuse three SEO signals** — GSC impressions, Ahrefs `site-explorer-organic-keywords` (rank data, Alexander's tool doesn't have this), and the client's product catalog (specific product names → specific prompts like *"La Vie Est Belle Intense avis"* that nobody else can reach from query data alone).

Pipeline:
1. Pull GSC top queries (when ownership available) + Ahrefs organic keywords.
2. Detect intent (branded / commercial / comparison / transactional / informational) via rule-based classifier with stopword filtering.
3. Match against a per-client `known_products` catalog → route to specialized templates ("Que vaut le parfum X de Lancôme en 2026 ?").
4. Deduplicate against existing Peec prompts (Jaccard on content tokens).
5. Propose new topics where categorization gaps exist.
6. Batch create via Peec `create_prompts` + `create_topics` (dry-run safe by default).

### Real run — Lancôme

Starting state: 57 existing prompts, 1 topic ("Longévité"), 123 competitors tracked.

After Module 1 dry-run: **22 new prompts across 6 proposed topics** (Parfums, Maquillage Yeux, Maquillage Teint, Soins Sérums, Soins Crèmes, Soins Diagnostic). Top proposal consolidated from 7 keyword variants: *"Que vaut le parfum La Vie Est Belle de Lancôme en 2026 ?"* with combined traffic 19 096/mo.

Full output: [`outputs/lancome_m1_dry_run.csv`](outputs/lancome_m1_dry_run.csv)
